For Players

Success Plan

At Sno-King Junior Golf, Coach Gerry coaches players to learn playing at the higher personal par. Here are few tips on what you can do to survive and thrive.

To score in the low 80's to 70's you need to schedule a practice or play every week. Golf is a game of confidence. Go out practice and play as often as you can.

Parents

  • >
    Encourage, Encourage and Encourage. Golf is the hardest game to play.
  • >
    "Love for the game". We play it because it is a fun game.
  • >
    Ask questions. Lots of questions.
  • >
    Track your players practice and play.

 

Go To Practice

  • >
    Sounds easy, but the temptation to skip practice is great.
  • >
    A big part of success in improving is just showing up-so show up and be on time.

 

Focus, engage.

  • >
    In lessons: engage, pay attention and think about the practice/lesson of the day.
  • >
    Relax, Really. Most common error in golf is a distracted swing.
  • >
    Commit!!! "If you are in-doubt get out and reset".
  • >
    It takes less than two seconds to swing the club. Focus. Give it your full attention.

 

Be proactive. Jump in.

  • >
    Ask questions.
  • >
    Talk to your Coach.
  • >
    Do it your own way: in person or email. Quick question after class/practice.
  • >
    Make a plan: formulate one good question for each class each week.

 

Practice on your own.

  • >
    After the golf season ends, go to the driving range and practice.
  • >
    Play with your friends and family.

In Partnership with the Parents

Sno-King Junior Golf develop better golfers in partnership with the parents. Together we can build their future. Not every junior golfers play at college golf and or play at the pro level, but if we can teach them early on how to live a life's core values and commit to living a healthy life style they could grow up to be better citizens

How to Help Your Player

Value School First, Golf Second
  • >
    Encourage, encourage, and encourage your player. Golf is very difficult to play and it will test the player's patience and discipline.
  • >
    Provide the best golf equipment you can afford.
  • >
    Bring healthy snacks.
  • >
    Do extra practice/play on your own.
  • >
    Practice or play with us.
  • >
    Provide carpool transportation.

 

Make Donations

Donation of golf equipment, training aid materials, books in good condition are welcomed. Fundraising for equipment, training aid materials, and players who are eligible to compete at the higher level would be very appreciated.

Lesson Types

Personal Par

Goal: Play Scramble
Learn to Learn

Student will be shown, hear, read, write and do the basic golf fundamental of full swing, short game and putting. Student will be taught proper technique of POSTURE, SWING MECHANIC, RULES and ETIQUETTE

Bogey

Goal: Play on your own
Learn to Train

Student will be train to adopt the basic golf fundamental skill into their own natural POSTURE, SWING MECHANIC, PRE-SHOT ROUTINE and FOCUS. On the course training student will practice RULES and ETIQUETTE

Par

Goal: Break 100
Learn to Train

Student will train to practice regularly. Learn to do practice and play notes, work on weakness part of the game. Rehearse fundamentals of swing, short game and putting. To make the golf style instinctive and natural

Birdie

Goal: Break 90
Learn to Complete

Student work on FOCUS shot precession yardages. Train to get up and down, lag putting and keep track of shots. Develop a "Game Plan" and practice mental toughness.

Eagle

Goal: Break 80
Compete to Finish Strong

Play with confidence, follow SNO-KING golf rules of relax and have fun, hit fairways, avoid hazards, green in regulations and no three putting. Practice for consistency, and physical/mental endurance. Proper equipment, diet and fitness exercise.

Ace

Goal: To finish 1st
Play to Win

Physically and mentally prepared. Excel in all 5 areas of the game (course management, tee box, approach shot, around the green, and putting). Execute game plan, handle pressure and play for the love of the game
